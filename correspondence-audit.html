Correspondence Audit — IRS Letter Explained

What This Is

A correspondence audit is the most common type of IRS audit and is conducted entirely by mail. It usually focuses on one or two items, such as income, credits, or deductions.

Even though it is handled by mail, deadlines and documentation requirements are still strict.

What Happens If You Ignore It

  • The IRS may disallow the item under review
  • A tax adjustment may be finalized
  • Penalties and interest may apply
