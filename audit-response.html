What This System Does

1. Audit Type & Scope Identification

Determines whether your notice is a correspondence audit, office audit, field audit, or document request. Identifies tax years and items under examination.

2. What the IRS Is Requesting

Extracts specific items requested and establishes scope limits to prevent over-disclosure.

3. What You Should and Should NOT Provide

Explicit guidance on document handling with default to SUMMARIZE, not ATTACH. Strong warnings against bulk uploads.

4. Response Preparation Strategy

Allowed and prohibited actions with scope limits enforced.

5. Escalation Risk & When to Stop

Hard stop conditions where professional representation is required.

6. Audit-Appropriate Response Outline

Structured outline (not complete response) with minimal narrative.

7. Professional Representation Advisory

Clear guidance on when to seek professional help.

⚠️ This System Will STOP You If:

  • Field audit detected (requires immediate professional representation)
  • Multi-year audit (2+ years under examination)
  • Dollar exposure exceeds $25,000
  • IRS requests in-person or phone interview
  • Audit scope is expanding beyond original notice
  • Notice contains criminal investigation language
  • 30-day or 90-day letter (formal examination notice)

Message you'll see: "This is the point at which professional representation is strongly recommended."
