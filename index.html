GENERATED RESPONSE (EXCERPT):

"This letter is submitted in response to IRS Notice CP2000 regarding reported income discrepancies for the 2023 tax year.

Supporting documentation is available upon request.

Notice Type: 1099-K Payment App Income

IRS NOTICE (INPUT):

"Third-party payment processors reported $18,400 in income. This amount does not appear on your tax return. Proposed tax due: $3,220."

SYSTEM ANALYSIS:

  • 1099-K reporting discrepancy from payment processor (Venmo/PayPal/CashApp)
  • Reported amount: $18,400
  • Proposed tax liability: $3,220
  • Likely includes personal reimbursements or non-taxable transfers

GENERATED RESPONSE (EXCERPT):

"This letter is submitted in response to IRS Notice regarding third-party payment processor reporting for the 2023 tax year.

The reported 1099-K amount of $18,400 includes personal reimbursements and non-taxable transfers between family members. These transactions do not constitute taxable income.

A detailed breakdown of the transactions is available upon request.

We respectfully request that the IRS review this clarification and adjust the proposed tax liability accordingly."

Notice Type: Schedule C Audit Request

IRS NOTICE (INPUT):

"Your Schedule C business income and expenses for 2023 have been selected for examination. Please provide documentation for all reported business expenses totaling $42,300."

SYSTEM ANALYSIS:

  • Schedule C examination notice for business expenses
  • Total expenses under review: $42,300
  • Documentation required for substantiation
  • Response deadline: 30 days from notice date

GENERATED RESPONSE (EXCERPT):

"This letter is submitted in response to the examination notice regarding Schedule C business expenses for the 2023 tax year.

The reported business expenses of $42,300 are substantiated by receipts, invoices, and bank statements. These expenses are ordinary and necessary for business operations.

Documentation is organized by expense category and available for review.

We respectfully request confirmation of the examination process and submission requirements."

IRS Letter Types We Handle

What is a CP2000 Notice?

A CP2000 notice means the IRS found a discrepancy between the income reported on your tax return and information they received from third parties. This common notice requires a careful response within 30 days to avoid additional penalties and interest. Our AI analyzes your specific CP2000 and generates the appropriate response.

Common IRS Letters Explained

  • CP2000: Proposed changes to your tax return (income discrepancy)
  • CP2501: Request for information about income
  • 1099-K: Payment card and third-party transactions
  • CP3219A: Statutory notice of deficiency
  • Letter 525: General 30-day letter
  • Letter 566: Initial contact for examination
